One way data travels the internet is in "packets". These packets will travel from one machine to another until they reach their destination.

The relationship between an IP address and DNS are they both are assigned to a server. An IP address is an adress assigned to any computer so that it can be identified on a network, while a DNS is a domain name service that converts alphabetical references into a servers IP.

A cookie is a small piece of data sent from a website and stored on the users computer by the users web browser while they are browsing.

Three basic parts of a URL are the protocol, domain, and subdomain.
